3. **Matplotlib companion to `branch_compare.py`** *(landed; commit `f1e67f7`; `scripts/plot_branches.py`)*. Per-trajectory H-trace / `logit_gap` / `gap_over_H` overlays for the 25-branch corpus on common axes; grid figures by outcome and by regime tag. First-class empirical instrument rather than paint-on-glass — visual inspection of joint structure (phase-shift relative to parent's cycle, transient-then-resync vs. immediate-divergence, joint amplitude / gap recovery patterns) is expected to surface readings the scalar classifier misses by construction. Read-only over the substrate; no GPU.

4. **Regime-tag consolidation.** Lift regime-tag derivation out of `scripts/shape_catalog.py`'s inline rules and `scripts/noperiod_audit.py`'s script-local A/B split into a canonical `peirce/regime.py` resolver — a function from substrate primitives, not a manifest. Audit script landed (`scripts/regime_tag_audit.py`); planning at `notes/regime-tag-consolidation.md`. Audit findings (see freshness stamp) put two design questions on the table: structured-vs-flat tag (HARMONIC orthogonal to slot/scaffold strongly implies structured); HARMONIC predicate tightness (strict harmonic ladder catches a different population than the N1 annotator's looser reading — both are defensible; the resolver pins one). Once the resolver lands, consumer scripts (`shape_catalog`, `first_batch_branches.BATCH` column, `branch_compare`, `plot_branches`) refactor to call it; manifest tag-column in `BATCH` becomes audit ground-truth, not source of truth. Both move 6 (transient window) and move 7 (burn-in revision) benefit directly from the canonical regime-tag surface; move 5 (completion-space) benefits because "what regimes are reachable from this position at temperature T" becomes a clean readout once tag-derivation is automatic.

5. **Completion-space enumeration at low T (per-exemplar).** The deepest project-distinctive thread surfaced this cycle. Reframe: at T=0, exactly one completion; at infinitesimal T, the same completion almost surely; at low-but-finite T, divergence is possible only at positions where `T_req ≤ T_budget` — exactly the "accessible bifurcation points" under standard sampling at that temperature. Exhaustive enumeration of the completion tree at fixed T for a single exemplar (every accessible branch taken; each branch recursively the same treatment) maps the *accessible-attractor neighbourhood at sampling-temperature budget T* — an observable expressed in sampling-strategy vocabulary that connects the project's perturbation primitive to the decoding literature without going through the basin-as-initial-condition-partition framing the prior cycle retired. Subsumes (a) per-position K-fan-out via Gumbel-Top-k sampling-without-replacement (Kool 2019; see `lit-review.md` addendum) and (b) lower-rank alt-token selection (rank-K for K > 2) as operational components rather than separate moves. Cost dominated by KV-prefill (~157s per branch in N2), so the pilot stays small — 1-2 exemplars at 1-2 T values; observed tree geometry informs whether wider runs are warranted. Prefix-shared KV cache across siblings (the B3 schema deferred in `design-reqs.md`) is the natural optimisation if scale demands it. Engine work + design; a `step_distribution(...)` runner helper (currently aspirational in `peirce/README.md`) is on the implementation path.

6. **Transient-window perturbation batch.** Tests the `design-reqs.md` *transient leverage* question that the first batch did not touch — perturbation at positions before the trajectory has settled into its attractor regime. Selection rule, alt-token rule, and classifier carry over from move 2's first batch; the new axis is position-along-trajectory (transient window) rather than alt-token-selection-strategy. Complements move 5's deep-window completion-space picture with the pre-burn-in regime.

7. **Burn-in threshold revision for `DEEP_START`.** Substrate-contract pass. Current `DEEP_START = 1024` is fixed against `L_arch = 2047`, arbitrary against the trajectories themselves; under monotone T=0 stability the principled move is to thresh on the trajectory's own settling — e.g., per-trajectory `max(onset + margin, some_floor)` where `onset` is the existing first-position-with-entropy-below-0.1-for-8-steps primitive. Implications: catalog scalar values (`floor_H`, `osc_amp`, `peaks`, `period`, `gap_over_H`) re-derive on the slow-collapsing tail of the population; regime tag-set largely robust but borderline classifications could flip; branch-selection candidate pool shrinks for late-settling trajectories. Lands as a single deliberate revision once moves 4-6 data is in hand and the regime tags' robustness to the revision can be measured directly. Companion: `design-reqs.md` "leverage candidate" language softening graduates from `ideas.md` in the same revision pass; the C2 branching protocol's mechanics stand, the axis naming gets the data-informed rewording.

## Triggers

- **Branch-switch announced.** Re-read this CLAUDE.md and any path-specific CLAUDE.md for the new branch. Run `git log <other>..HEAD` both directions to surface divergence; raise rebase questions when substantive doc or spec changes have landed.
- **HEAD past Last-reviewed stamp on entry.** Skim commits since the stamp before substantive action; flag drift between the working picture encoded here and the current code/docs.
- **Wrapping a logical unit (commit, observation entry, design pivot).** Decide whether anything non-obvious surfaced that should land in memory.
- **Approaching /compact.** Triage in-flight state by scope. Project-level (cross-branch truth) → memory. Branch-level (commit progress, in-flight design state on a feature branch) → commit message, observation entry pinned to commit, or the branch's own CLAUDE.md if a handoff note is genuinely needed.
- **Hint of a re-founding event** — a finding that doesn't fit the foundation, or accumulating drift between foundation and active understanding. Surface deliberately rather than absorb silently. Re-foundings are normal and discrete; the discipline is to mark the cycle boundary, archive the prior cycle's working surface, tag the prior-cycle commit for reproducibility, and start the new cycle fresh.
